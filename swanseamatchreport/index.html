Chelsea Vs Swansea: Match Report

A dominant performance from the blues resulted in a dissapointing 2-2 draw away to the Swans at the Liberty Stadium. The Blues edged out the home team for the first 15 minutes of the game before Diego COsta struck home Oscar's lay off in the bottom right of Fabianski's goal 18 minutes in. The blues dominated for the rest of the half with Diego Costa striking across goal and wide after John Terry nodded on a Willian corner.
The blues dominance was met with frequent counter attacks from the hosts and a long ball forward by Leroy Fer was met by Barrow to cross it over tempting Courtois of his goal line to the edge of the 18 yard box before he comitted a foul just inside the area on the onrushing Sigurdsson racing to get on the end of Barrow's cross. A yellow card and penalty later and the hosts were back on level terms with Sigurdsson coverting the spot kick. Swansea continued to press the blues defence and 2 minutes after the penalty Leroy Fer took the ball off Gary Cahil and bundled it over the line to give the swans a 2-1 lead with just under half an hour to go. Replays show that Gary Cahill was clearly fouled in the build up to the goal.
Changes from Conte saw Willian and Matic replaced my Moses and Fabregas. The blues kept pressing before Ivanovic burst down the line. His cross was deflected up and sat up beautifully for Diego Costa to get up and over sending his bycicle kick into the net via a deflection from Fernandez. The blues continued to press but couldn't find a winner in the last 9 minutes. The game finished 2-2 leaving Chelsea second 2 points of leaders Manchester City.
